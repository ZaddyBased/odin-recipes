Apple Risotto Recipe

The What, Where, Why

Apple Risotto is an italian dish native to Trentino-South Tyrol, a region in northern Italy. In Food Wars, the main character is facing off his dad for the 490th time and needs a light nutritious meal to wow the judges. He improvises, and they are amazed at his dish. Even though he lost, this shows what you can do with what you have while still being creative.

What You Need

  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ Cup diced onions
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups of Granny Smith apples, peeled and cubed
  • ⅓ cup of dry white wine
  • 2 tbsp freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • 8 strips bacon

The How

  1. Boil the chicken broth and then adjust to heat to bring it to a summer. Set aside.
  2. Add 2 tbsp. of butter and ½ cup of onions over low heat for 3 minutes. Then add in the rice and 1 cup of apples and sauté for 3 minutes. Mix in the wine and bring to a boil.
  3. Add the hot broth in, just covering the rice, and keep stirring until it's absorbed. keep adding more broth in ½ cup increments and stir until fully absorbed.
