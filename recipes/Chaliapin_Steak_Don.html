Chaliapin Steak Don

The What, Where, & Why

Chaliapin was created by a russian opera singer named Feodor Chaliapin. He was having toothaches and needed a tender steak, so his chef obliged his his order, creating the chaliapin steak. Donburi is a Japanese "rice-bowl dish" consisting of fish, meat, vegetable or other ingredients simmered together and served over rice. The Chaliapin Steak Don is a creation from the anime Food Wars, and because of the message behind the meal i would like to show you how to make it.

What You Need

  • 250g Sirloin Steak
  • 1½ Onions, diced
  • 300g short-grain Japanese rice, cooked
  • 1 tablespoon picked plum paste (umeboshi)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Potato Starch
  • Green onions, chopped

The How

  1. Mince the onion. Tenderize the steak, then cover with chopped onions. Rest it for an hour. Then remove onions and sprinkle the meat with salt and pepper.
  2. Melt butter in a skillet and sauté the onions. Add salt and pepper to your liking. Set aside the onions.
  3. Add some cooking oil to the skillet. Cook the beef till golden brown on both sides. Remove the beef and let it rest on the side.
  4. Add red wine to the skillet, mixing with meat juice. Once reduced, add the onions again and sauté. Then, add butter, soy sauce and potato starch. Let the flavours blend into a sauce.
  5. Next, mix umeboshi with cooked rice. then, slice the steak meat into bite-sized pieces. Arrange the slices on your rice.
  6. Finally, pour the sauce with onions on the top of your Chaliapin steak and rice. Sprinkle chopped green onion with your donburi. Serve and enjoy while it's hot!
